-   **Assessment Date**: [Date of the assessment]

## 2. Risk Identification

Identify all potential risks that may affect the project or system. Risks can be categorized as:

-   **Technical Risks**: (e.g., technology incompatibility, security vulnerabilities, software bugs, hardware failure)
-   **Operational Risks**: (e.g., human errors, process failures, resource shortages)
-   **Environmental Risks**: (e.g., network outage, service provider failure)
-   **Financial Risks**: (e.g., budget overrun, unexpected costs)
-   **Schedule Risks**: (e.g., delivery delays)

| # | Risk Description | Category | Potential Cause | Potential Impact |
|---|------------------|----------|-----------------|------------------|
| 1 | [Example: Database failure] | Technical | Server crash/Misconfiguration | Data loss, Service outage |
| 2 | [Example: Budget overrun] | Financial | Inaccurate cost estimation | Project halt, Resource shortage |

## 3. Risk Analysis

For each identified risk, analyze its likelihood and severity of impact.

-   **Likelihood**:
    -   **Low**: Unlikely to occur.
    -   **Medium**: May occur occasionally.
    -   **High**: Very likely to occur.
-   **Impact**:
    -   **Minor**: Slight impact, easily manageable.
    -   **Moderate**: Noticeable impact, requires effort to manage.
    -   **Major**: Significant impact, may threaten project success.

| # | Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Risk Score (Likelihood × Impact) |
|---|------|------------|--------|----------------------------------|
| 1 | Database failure | High | Major | Very High |
| 2 | Budget overrun | Medium | Moderate | Medium |

*   **Risk Score**: Can be calculated by multiplying a numerical value for likelihood (e.g., Low=1, Medium=2, High=3) by a numerical value for impact (e.g., Minor=1, Moderate=2, Major=3).

## 4. Risk Response

For each high-rated risk, define a response strategy.

-   **Avoid**: Change the project plan to eliminate the risk entirely.
-   **Mitigate**: Take actions to reduce the likelihood or impact of the risk.
-   **Transfer**: Shift the risk to a third party (e.g., insurance).
-   **Accept**: Acknowledge the risk and deal with it if it occurs.

| # | Risk | Response Strategy | Proposed Actions | Owner | Status |
|---|------|-------------------|------------------|-------|--------|
| 1 | Database failure | Mitigate | Daily backup, Continuous monitoring | DevOps Team | In Progress |
| 2 | Budget overrun | Mitigate | Weekly budget review, Expense tracking | Project Manager | In Progress |
