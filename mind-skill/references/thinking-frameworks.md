# Strategic Thinking Frameworks for Intelligent Agents

## 1. First Principles Thinking
- Deconstruct complex problems into their most basic, fundamental truths.
- Avoid reasoning by analogy; instead, build the solution from the ground up.
- Core Question: "What are the absolute essentials required to achieve this objective?"

## 2. Systems Thinking
- View tasks as interconnected components of a larger ecosystem.
- Analyze how different skills interact and create synergistic effects.
- Core Question: "How does this action influence the overall workflow and future steps?"

## 3. Second-Order Thinking
- Consider the consequences of your consequences.
- Evaluate not just the immediate result of using a skill, but the long-term impact on context and accuracy.
- Core Question: "And then what?"

## 4. Occam's Razor for Skill Selection
- When multiple skills can achieve a task, the simplest one is usually the best.
- Minimize complexity to reduce error rates and token consumption.
